How to waste your weekend while not wasting it...

  I tried to be productive this weekend but here's how luck had me. In this post, I am to explain you how a simple issues turned into a nightmare.
  

SATURDAY:


Objective: Interface ESP32 based hardware to Cloud Database and establish Communication

ME: Building the Code, Eliminating Errors, Compiling, Testing ....DONE
  Let's Upload it to ESP32. Should be very Simple.

LUCK: Uploading Fails. ESP32 Not Entering Boot Mode
  Checking the schematic, Found it Doesn't have RC Filter Capacitor on Reset Pin.

ME: Easy Fix, I will do it myself.
   Okay Soldered the Cap, Things worked, Upload DONE

The Real Nightmare Begins...

LUCK: MCU Freezes, The sketch doesn't run.
  ESP32 connects and disconnects back again in a loop. I am unable to upload again.
   ERROR: Serial port Busy...

ME: Searching Internet: Okay, The USB Hub cannot provide current needed for ESP32 WiFi.begin()
  See how much current Can it deliver. Dell doesnt say a word about that :(
Tried several Power Supplies. No Luck.
Measure the Current, Oh its 34mA. Yes for Sure Hub can provide that.

ME: Let's try a simpler program. Blink should work but Serial doesn't connect. How Can I upload?
  After several hours, Force boot ESP32 in Reset Mode. Yes it connects. Blink upload successful and working.

ME: Let's try WiFi.begin() now. Again Freezes.
   Let's try my other ESP32 Board. Another Freeze. Doesnt even connect back no matter what I did.

Clueless Problems


ME: Reinstall Drivers. Restart PC. Clear USB Event log.
  Oh the USB device is blocked
   Error: Device Descriptor Request Failed...

ME: Okay, CP2102 is the USB-Serial Tranceiver. Read CP2102 Datasheet, Force Hardware Reset, No Luck No matter what I do: CP2102 on one ESP32 board is now bricked. :(

ME: It might be my PC. Internet Says: Clear Device ID from HKEY_LOCAL Machines, Disable USB Active Suspend from Advanced Power Settings.

LUCK: No Worries. I'm here for You.... :(

  

SUNDAY:


Another Wrong Turn.....

ME: Maybe its my PC. Let's try another Computer.
  Okay, I Have Raspberry Pi but my Monitor is Busy with PC. SSH should work

LUCK: You Don't Even Know the IP. Network Admin Won't let you know Either...

ME: Let's Try nmap scan to get Raspberry Pi IP Need to install nmap, Download Mirror speed is Terrible 6kb/s, Download Failed.
Try Again
   Okay, Installed. Let's Try nmap scan.

LUCK: Unsuccessful Run, Turns out my VPN is blocking, Turn Off VPN, Still no Luck. Oh! The network restricts nmap unless admin.
ME: No way to ssh, Let's Disconnect all my monitor setup and Connect PI to HDMI display. Oops, the installed OS is CLI only.

LUCK: It has to do System Updates before you could do anything..
Okay, Updates Done. After lsusb, its confirmed your ESP32 Board have a bricked CP2102 bridge.

It doesn't END here.


ME:
Reading CP2102 Datasheet, Let's Try Hardware Reset for CP2102, try Changing Strapping pins.... Still No Luck.

LUCK: Oh, You messed the Solder on QFN Pads while placing Multimeter probs.
  Now there is a short between VBUS and RESET Pin.

ME: Taking it to Phone repair shop, Getting fresh reflow to fix the short.

LUCK:
   Still The same issue. Connect / Disconnect Loop iterated with while(1) .... :)

ME: Let's bypass CP2102 and perform SPI Flash.
   Oh, I think ESP32 is resetting in loop and not the CP2102. But why serial doesn't connect?

LUCK: SPI Flash Process FAILED. Not Entering boot Mode.

What's the Problem?


ME: Okay, My One ESP32 Devkit is Dead Now. The Other one doesn't flash without until Forced Reset.
  I will try restoring with AT Factory Firmware someday.

ME: Okay, Luck! Listen, You wasted my whole weekend. I'm still where I was two days ago.
  Now Please tell me what's wrong.

LUCK: Connect ESP32 to a DC power adapter. Observe the status LEDs

ME:
Ohhh, It works. No Longer Reset Loops. But Why it doesn't work with PC?
  Is it lack of current? But PC doesn'r Give any warnings about current surge either.

Finally Fixed....


Turns out, It was a bad USB cable. Bought a new cable and everything worked like a charm.

  • ESP32 Has current surge upto 700mA at WiFi.begin()

  • USB Cable had poor Copper Wires for Power

  • Not Enough current -> Drop in Voltage -> ESP32 Reset -> Again & Again


    • ...............


      I always curse myself for solving simple problems, the Harder Way
    But this is the problem, that You don't even know what is the problem :)
    Let's Hope The next week would be better...

    "When you want to know how things really work, study them when they're coming apart."

    William Gibson, Zero History
